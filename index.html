Optical Design

I design astronomical spectrographs and camera mostly Precise Radial Velocity (PRV) Instruments for exoplanets characterization in different wavelength: UV, Optical, NIR. My previous work in Chile with Prof. Leonardo Vanzi includes FIDEOS, TARdYS and PLATOSPEC.

Optomechanical Design

This is where I started my work in the field of astronomical instrumentation. I designed the optomechanical supports for the IGRINS mirror mounts and perform finite element analysis. Then I transfer my experience to the design and construction of TARdYS.

Alignment

Simulations

Together with the spectrograph design, I made a simulation platform for sentivity analysis and exposure time calculator. This simulation platform is highly robust that it can be tuned to work with different spectrographs at different wavelength range. Figure shows well agreement between simulation and observation in case of FIDEOS, an optical spectrograph.

Fabry Perot Interferometer

This is the most recent project I am working on. With the FIOS team at Harvard, we are developing an instrument concept for an ultra-high resolution application to study exoplanet atmospheres using transmission spectroscopy and a cross correlation techniques.

Projects

List of the recent projects which included some my work at different phase of each project.

IGRINS

An Immersion Grating near infrared (H&K band) high resolution (R~40,000) spectrograph.

FIDEOS

A dual fiber high resolution (R~40,000) visible spectrgraph at La Silla 1 m telescope.

TARdYS

A Tao-Aiuc high resolution (R~66,000) near infrared Y-band spectrograph planned for the University of Tokyo Atacama 6.5 m telescope.

PLATOSPEC

A high resolution (R~70,000) UV spectrograph for a ESO 1.5 m telescope planned to be a follow-up instrument for PLATO.

FIOS

An ultra-high resolution booster (R~600,000) based on Fabry Perot Interferometer for the next generation Extremely Large Telescopes, can be used for a focus study of exoplanets' biomarkers.
